Our Story

Built on the belief that everyone deserves financial confidence

Brightside Finance Foundation is a comprehensive initiative designed to empower students and adults with essential financial literacy skills. By providing accessible tools, interactive workshops, and tailored resources, Brightside equips individuals to confidently manage budgets, navigate financial challenges, and build a strong foundation for long-term financial independence.

// Our Journey

How we got here

2012 — Founded
A kitchen table idea becomes a nonprofit

Co-founders Dr. Amara Osei and James Whitfield launch Brightside out of a shared frustration: their students were graduating high school without ever learning to balance a budget or understand a paycheck.

2014 — First Partnership
Chicago Public Schools adopts our curriculum

After a successful pilot in 3 schools, CPS partners with Brightside to bring Money Sprouts and Smart Start to 42 schools across the district, reaching over 8,000 students in year one.

2017 — Expansion
Adult programs launch nationwide

We extend our curriculum beyond schools with Launch Pad and Peak Years — community workshop programs for adults. Our first year serves 4,200 participants across 12 cities.

2020 — Going Digital
Full online platform launches during COVID-19

When the pandemic closes in-person workshops, we rapidly build a full digital learning platform. Enrollment surges 340% as people across the country seek financial guidance during economic uncertainty.

2023 — Medical Finance Initiative
Launching specialized healthcare financial literacy

Recognizing that medical debt is the #1 cause of bankruptcy in America, we launch a dedicated Medical Finances program — covering insurance, HSAs, hospital billing, and emergency savings.

Today
48,200 lives and counting

With 127 partner organizations, 6 specialized programs, and a growing team of volunteers and educators, Brightside continues to expand its reach — with a goal of 100,000 participants by 2027.
